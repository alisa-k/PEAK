Matter includes all solids, liquid, and gases.
The amount of matter in a object is the object's mass.
Mass can be measured.
Objects with more mass have more weight.
Volume is the amount of space taken up by an object.
It can also be measured.
The amount of mass in a given volume is an object's density.
Physical properties of matter can be observed without changing the substance.
Another physical property of matter is state.
Matter can be a solid, liquid, or a gas.
Physical changes to matter changes its size, shape, or state without forming a new substance.
